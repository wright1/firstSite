Falling in Love with code...begrudingly!

I have heard people confess their undeniable love for coding, they have a 'love at first sight' experience with coding. I am envious. My relationship with coding is no where near as clear cut. We're in a good place at the moment, but that may not last. Our relationship is a collection of experiences, I try not to filter those experience into 'good' and 'bad'. However, there was a time that we were out and out enemies for the following reasons:

Coding is Judgemental

Imagine you had a friend that called out every single mistake you made, every single time with a large red error message, how long do you think that relationship will last? Well, speaking for myself I can say that relationship would not see the dawn of day! NEXT! May I request a friend with a little more compassion and understanding and a little less judgement please, thank you very much.

Coding kept stalking me

Many times I tried to leave Coding. I didn't feel good enough, every time I tried to make this relationship work error messages were thrown. So like any sensible gal I decided to leave. Find something new.

But Coding wouldn't leave me alone. I would be sitting down to my favourite breakfast of porriage oats topped with fresh banana and strawberries when Coding would pop up, 'we can make this work, maybe if you tried an array.....' On my way to work I would find myself trying to figure out how the 'this' keyword works. However hard I tried to walk away Coding was there.

Something Changed

A simple act made a drastic change to my relationship with Coding. I did something I hadn't done before...I actually READ the error messages I was receiving. Yes, I know, Radical!

You see, at a glance error messages seemed ineligible to me, like something written in a foreign language. It didn't occur to me that the message was trying to communicate something helpful, maybe even useful to me.

I had no idea there were different 'types' of error messages. Maybe I was getting Coding all wrong. Maybe what I was calling judgement was actually support and what I termed stalking was actually persistence, an unwillingness to give up. And it was in this realisation that our relationship turned a corner.
So, lesson learnt. Communication is key. Read the Error messages, they could save your relationship!
